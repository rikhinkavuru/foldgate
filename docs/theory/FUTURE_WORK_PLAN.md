## 1. D1 — Ensemble disagreement as a certifiable label-free lower bound on the concept gap

#### Problem & why it is open

The project's Theorem 1 proves that any label-free certificate built from covariate-measurable weights recovers only the source conditional $\eta_P(x)=\Pr_P(Y=1\mid X=x)$, so the realized target selective risk on the accept region is $R_Q(\tau_c)=R_{\mathrm{ref}}(\tau_c)+\bar\Delta_c$, and the accept-region **concept gap** $\bar\Delta_c$ is invariant to the weights. No covariate-weighted certificate can *detect* $\bar\Delta_c$; it silently under-reports realized risk.

D1's claim is that cross-model pose disagreement yields a **one-sided lower bound** on accept-region selective risk that is *not* forbidden by Theorem 1. The reason is precise. Disagreement is a deterministic function of the input complex $X$ (each frozen model's pose $x_m(X)$ is fixed given $X$), so it is fully **covariate-measurable** and does not "escape the information set." What Theorem 1 forbids is a certified *upper* bound on risk (safety) obtained by reweighting the source conditional. A disagreement floor is a certified *lower* bound on risk (danger), built by referencing the unobserved crystal pose $y^\star$ through the triangle inequality, and Theorem 1 places no constraint on lower bounds. The reconciliation rests entirely on the lower-versus-upper-bound distinction.

The structured-output setting supplies the mechanism: pose labels are thresholded distances $Y_m=\mathbf 1\{\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,y^\star)\le\rho\}$, $\rho=2\,\text{Å}$, in a metric space, so the triangle inequality converts geometric disagreement into a guaranteed error count. This conversion is not assumption-free: it holds inside a single common frame that contains $y^\star$, and the deployment-time frame-transfer slack is handled explicitly below (T1 assumptions and Method step 1).

The empirical phenomenon is old and robust. Consensus docking (Houston & Walkinshaw 2013) established that requiring multiple programs to agree filters wrong poses (single-program success ~55-64% rises to 82%+). Co-folding memorisation studies (bioRxiv 2025.02.03.636309; Nat. Commun. s41467-025-63947-5) show accuracy is almost perfectly predicted by training-set similarity, i.e. errors are shift-driven and structured. What is new is not that disagreement lower-bounds error, and not the factor one-half on the pairwise-disagreement rate. Both are now published (see below). What is new is converting *metric* cross-model disagreement of a *continuous structured* output into an error floor by triangle inequality to a latent crystal pose, the multi-model packing form, the diverse-versus-consensus concept-gap decomposition, and the exact reconciliation with the project's covariate-weighted impossibility.

**Closest prior work and the honest differentiator.** The mechanism "if two models disagree, at least one is wrong, so the disagreement rate floors the error rate," including the exact factor one-half, is *already published in two independent lines*:

- **Incoherence as an Oracle-less Measure of Error** (arXiv:2507.00057, AAAI 2026) formalizes the pointwise inequality D1's T1 rests on, in the code-generation domain: disagreement between two programs on an input places a *sound* floor on the failure probability and "never produces false positives" (certified danger, never safety, the same one-sidedness as T1/T2). It uses **discrete I/O equality** (two outputs are equal or not).
- **Structured Credal Learning** (arXiv:2603.14070, 2026) proves a minimax lower bound $\inf\sup\mathbb E[L_Q-L_{P^\star}]\ge\tfrac12\eta^\star$ where $\eta^\star$ is the maximum pairwise disagreement rate, i.e. the *same* "one-half times pairwise disagreement floors error" factor T1's pairwise form rests on, and it does so explicitly in a distribution-shift / labeling-uncertainty regime (credal-set diameter equals maximum pairwise disagreement). It is classification / TV-distance only, on **discrete labels**, with no continuous-metric output and no geometric $2\rho$ trigger to a latent target.

D1 concedes both. The bare pairwise floor and its factor one-half are prior art. The differentiator that survives is narrow and specific: **(a)** a genuine metric on a *continuous* structured output, with the triangle inequality referencing the *unobserved crystal pose* $y^\star$ through a $2\rho$ geometric trigger set by the label radius (credal and incoherence work use discrete labels or I/O equality, never a distance-to-latent-target trigger); **(b)** the multi-model packing/clique floor; **(c)** the diverse-versus-consensus concept-gap **decomposition** and its consensus mini-impossibility; and **(d)** the exact reconciliation with the covariate-weighted Theorem 1. Items (c) and (d) are the theoretical headline. The pairwise inequality, the packing argument, and the finite-sample confidence bounds are routine machinery.

Other prior situated against D1:

- *Cross-Model Disagreement as a Label-Free Correctness Signal* (arXiv:2603.25450, 2026) — LLM-only empirical perplexity/entropy heuristic, no finite-sample bound, no metric geometry.
- *Dis²* disagreement discrepancy (arXiv:2306.00312, 2023) — an *upper* bound on target error via a *trained* critic and *source labels*. Opposite direction, requires training and labels.
- *GDE* (arXiv:2106.13799, 2021) — disagreement $\approx$ error as a two-sided *estimate* under calibration that fails under exactly this shift. Used only as a caveated tightening (T3), never as the certificate.
- *The Limits of Distribution-Free Conditional Predictive Inference* (arXiv:1903.04684, 2021) — the foundational impossibility Theorem 1 specializes; D1 respects it by certifying danger, never conditional safety.

**Honest scope statement carried into the write-up.** The "at least one is wrong" inequality and its factor one-half are elementary and already delivered by both arXiv:2507.00057 (discrete I/O) and arXiv:2603.14070 (classification shift). Wrapping a binary disagreement indicator in a one-sided Clopper-Pearson lower confidence bound is textbook binomial inference with no methodological novelty. The load-bearing contributions are therefore **(i)** the metric/triangle-inequality conversion of a continuous pose into a thresholded error count *against a latent target*, and the multi-model packing floor; **(ii)** the diverse-versus-consensus concept-gap **decomposition** and its consensus mini-impossibility; and **(iii)** the exact reconciliation with the covariate-weighted Theorem 1. Items (ii) and (iii) are the theoretical headline; the finite-sample certificate is routine machinery.

#### Target theorem(s)

**Setup and symbols.** $K$ frozen co-folding models index $m\in\{1,\dots,K\}$ (AF3, Boltz-1/2, Chai-1, Protenix). For target complex $i$, $x_m^{(i)}$ is model $m$'s top-ranked ligand pose in a **single fixed common frame** (every model's *protein* superposed once onto one reference receptor; see Method step 1); $y^{\star(i)}$ is the crystal pose; $\mathrm{RMSD}$ is symmetry-corrected ligand RMSD in that frozen frame (`spyrmsd`, `minimize=False`, so it is a genuine quotient metric under the ligand automorphism group and the triangle inequality holds). Correctness $Y_m^{(i)}=\mathbf 1\{\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m^{(i)},y^{\star(i)})\le\rho\}$, error $e_m^{(i)}=1-Y_m^{(i)}$, $\rho=2\,\text{Å}$. A deployed model $m_0$ has frozen score $s$ and per-stratum threshold $\tau_c$; accept region $A=\{s\ge\tau_c\}$, with $n_A$ accepted complexes. Define per-model target selective risk $R_m(\tau_c)=\Pr_Q(e_m=1\mid A)$, the **ensemble-mean risk** $\bar R(\tau_c)=\tfrac1K\sum_m R_m(\tau_c)$, and worst-model risk $R_{\max}(\tau_c)=\max_m R_m(\tau_c)$. $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}(\tau_c)=\tfrac1K\sum_m R_{\mathrm{ref},m}(\tau_c)$ is the mean of the per-model covariate-weighted label-free certificates Theorem 1 constrains. The **ensemble concept gap** is $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}=\bar R(\tau_c)-\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}(\tau_c)$. $n_g$ is the number of labelled target points in stratum $g$ (matching Theorem 3 notation).

**Important scope correction (why the certified object is the ensemble, not $m_0$).** Disagreement is symmetric and cannot assign blame to a specific model. The pairwise inequality below bounds only the *average* of a pair's risks, and the packing form bounds only the *mean over all $K$ models*. It is therefore invalid to certify $R_{m_0}$ (or a per-$m_0$ concept gap) from disagreement. Counterexample: if $m_0$ is always correct on $A$ ($R_{m_0}=0$) while two other models err in mutually $>2\rho$ directions, the disagreement statistic fires yet $R_{m_0}=0$. Assigning error to a *named* model label-free is provably impossible (Corollary T2c), so **all certified quantities below are stated for $\bar R$ and $R_{\max}$**, and the concept gap is defined against $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$.

**T1 (metric floor).** For any two models $m,m'$ and every point, in the single common frame,
$$\mathbf 1\{\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,x_{m'})>2\rho\}\le e_m+e_{m'}.$$
Taking conditional expectation over $A$,
$$\tfrac12\big(R_m(\tau_c)+R_{m'}(\tau_c)\big)\ \ge\ \tfrac12\,\Pr_Q\big(\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,x_{m'})>2\rho\mid A\big),$$
so for **this single fixed pair** $R_{\max}(\tau_c)\ge\tfrac12\Pr_Q(\text{that pair disagrees}\mid A)$. The factor one-half is valid only for one pre-registered pair; it does **not** carry over to a max-over-pairs statistic (see T2).

**Multi-model packing form (exact).** Build per target the graph $G$ on the $K$ poses with an edge whenever pairwise $\mathrm{RMSD}>2\rho$. Correct poses (all within $\rho$ of $y^\star$, hence within $2\rho$ of each other) form an independent set, so $\#\text{correct}\le\alpha(G)$ and $\#\text{errors}\ge K-\alpha(G)$. Averaging over $A$,
$$\bar R(\tau_c)\ \ge\ 1-\tfrac1K\,\mathbb E_Q[\alpha(G)\mid A],$$
which is an **equality-tight identity in expectation up to the independent-set slack**, not just a pairwise bound. Because $\alpha(G)\ge\omega(G)-1$ is loose, we use $\alpha(G)$ directly, not $\omega(G)$.

*Proof strategy.* Triangle inequality: $\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,y^\star)+\mathrm{RMSD}(x_{m'},y^\star)\ge\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,x_{m'})$; if both correct the left side is $\le2\rho$, so separation $>2\rho$ forces $\ge1$ error. Conditional expectation over $A$; the independent-set argument for the packing form; $R_{\max}\ge$ pair-mean for the single-pair statement.

*Exact assumptions.* (1) $\mathrm{RMSD}$ is a genuine metric on ligand coordinates in one shared frame (symmetry-corrected RMSD with `minimize=False` after one fixed protein superposition satisfies this). (2) **The shared frame contains $y^\star$.** For *validation* this is the crystal frame and the assumption holds exactly. For *deployment* $y^\star$ is absent, so disagreement is computed in $m_0$'s receptor frame while the label is intrinsically crystal-frame; the two agree only under near-rigid cross-model protein agreement. We do not assume that agreement. Instead we bound the frame-transfer discrepancy: let $\varepsilon$ be the maximum inter-model pocket-superposition residual (the largest backbone-superposition RMSD when aligning any model's receptor onto $m_0$'s). Then $|\mathrm{RMSD}_{m_0}(x_m,x_{m'})-\mathrm{RMSD}_{\mathrm{xtal}}(x_m,x_{m'})|\le 2\varepsilon$, so counting a pair as disagreeing only when $\mathrm{RMSD}_{m_0}>2\rho+2\varepsilon$ keeps the floor one-sided-valid at deployment. This slack is measured (not assumed small) and its empirical distribution across models is reported. (3) The disagreement radius equals twice the label radius (holds for the pose label; does **not** transfer to affinity or categorical labels). No independence, calibration, or exchangeability.

*Where it fails.* Power, not validity. The $2\rho=4\,\text{Å}$ trigger (plus the $2\varepsilon$ deployment slack) fires only on gross disagreement; if accepted complexes rarely disagree by that much the floor is honest but near-zero. The frame choice is fixed a priori and logged.

**T2 (impossibility-routing certificate + decomposition — the headline).** Over the $n_A$ accepted target complexes we certify two objects, each with its correct finite-sample tool.

*Binary pairwise / union statistic.* Let $Z_i^{(m,m')}=\mathbf 1\{\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m^{(i)},x_{m'}^{(i)})>2\rho\}$ for one **pre-registered** pair, and $Z_i=\mathbf 1\{\exists\,m<m':\mathrm{RMSD}>2\rho\}$ (any pair disagrees). These are Bernoulli, so an exact one-sided Clopper-Pearson lower confidence bound $p_L(\delta)$ applies. Then with probability $\ge1-\delta$:
$$\text{(single fixed pair):}\quad R_{\max}(\tau_c)\ \ge\ \tfrac12\,p_L^{(m,m')},\qquad\qquad \text{(any-pair union):}\quad R_{\max}(\tau_c)\ \ge\ \tfrac1K\,p_L^{Z}.$$
The any-pair form uses the factor $1/K$, **not** $1/2$: from $\{Z=1\}\subseteq\{{\ge}1\text{ error among }K\}$ and $\Pr({\ge}1\text{ error}\mid A)\le\sum_m R_m\le K\,R_{\max}$. (The earlier displayed $R_{\max}\ge\tfrac12 p_L$ for the max-over-pairs $Z$ was **wrong**: a $K=3$ counterexample where exactly one uniformly-chosen model errs $>2\rho$ from two correct poses gives $p=1$ yet $R_{\max}=\tfrac13<\tfrac12$; the union bound $p/K=\tfrac13$ is tight.) If the pre-registered-pair form is replaced by a max over all $\binom{K}{2}$ pairs, the multiplicity is absorbed by a Bonferroni split of $\delta$.

*Packing floor (the headline certificate).* The estimand $\bar R(\tau_c)\ge 1-\tfrac1K\mathbb E_Q[\alpha(G)\mid A]$ requires an upper confidence bound on $\mathbb E_Q[\alpha(G)\mid A]$, where $\alpha(G)/K\in[1/K,1]$ is a **bounded-mean** quantity, not a Bernoulli proportion. Clopper-Pearson is *invalid* here. We use a bounded-mean upper confidence bound (Hoeffding, or the tighter empirical-Bernstein / Hoeffding-Bentkus UCB already in the project's RCPS stack) on $\alpha(G)/K$, giving with probability $\ge1-\delta$
$$\bar R(\tau_c)\ \ge\ 1-\widehat{\mathbb E}[\alpha(G)\mid A]/K\ -\ b(\delta,n_A),$$
with $b(\delta,n_A)$ the concentration slack from the chosen inequality. The certified $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}$ is this lower bound minus $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}(\tau_c)$.

Whenever the certified floor exceeds $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$, this **certifies a strictly positive ensemble concept gap** that Theorem 1 proves the covariate-weighted certificate cannot detect (Theorem 1 applies per model; averaging the per-model impossibilities shows $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$ cannot detect $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}$ either, while the disagreement floor lower-bounds it).

**Decomposition (T2b).**
$$\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}=\bar\Delta_c^{\text{diverse}}+\bar\Delta_c^{\text{consensus}},$$
where disagreement certifies only $\bar\Delta_c^{\text{diverse}}$ (erroneous models mutually $>2\rho$ apart); $\bar\Delta_c^{\text{consensus}}$ (all models share one wrong mode) is label-free-uncertifiable.

**Consensus mini-impossibility (T2c).** No label-free statistic can certify $\bar\Delta_c^{\text{consensus}}$, and no label-free statistic can assign the error to a named model. Both follow from a two-DGP indistinguishability argument: construct two data-generating processes with identical multi-model outputs $\{x_m\}$ but different $y^\star$ (one where the shared mode is correct, one where it is wrong), the same proof technology as Theorem 1's tower identity and the 1903.04684 conditional-inference impossibility.

*Exact assumptions.* All $K$ model outputs available at test time (true, you run every model); accepted complexes exchangeable so the binomial / bounded-mean bounds apply; the disagreement statistic is covariate-measurable (it is) but yields a *lower* bound referencing $y^\star$, which Theorem 1 (a safety/upper-bound impossibility) does not constrain.

*Where it fails.* Certifies danger only, never safety, consistent with Theorem 1, with 1903.04684, and with the one-sidedness of arXiv:2507.00057 / arXiv:2603.14070; sold as a feature, honestly. The consensus blind spot (T2c) coincides with the novel-chemotype memorisation regime (all models copy the same plausible-but-wrong scaffold pose): the floor reads $\approx0$ while true risk is high. This is stated as a proven corollary, not left for review to find. On finite-sample coverage specifically: to our current reading arXiv:2507.00057 and arXiv:2603.14070 report the floor as a population/point object; D1's only addition on *that* axis is the exact one-sided LCB and the bounded-mean UCB for the packing form, and we claim no more (to be confirmed against their final text at write-up).

**T3 (tightening — heuristic estimate, never a certificate).** The clean identity $\Pr(\text{disagree}\mid x)=2e(x)(1-e(x))$ is **false** and its closed-form inversion is dropped. Two correct poses are always within $2\rho$ (triangle inequality), but a single error need not produce disagreement (a wrong pose in $(\rho,2\rho]$ on the same side of $y^\star$ can sit $<2\rho$ from a correct one), and two errors may agree or disagree. The honest generative model for a pair is
$$\Pr(\text{disagree}\mid x)=\pi_1(x)\,2e(x)\big(1-e(x)\big)+\pi_2(x)\,e(x)^2,$$
with geometry-dependent, unknown conditional-disagreement probabilities $\pi_1(x),\pi_2(x)\in[0,1]$ (the zero-error term vanishes exactly). Because $\pi_1,\pi_2$ are unknown, $e(x)$ is not identified; we therefore (a) keep only the T1/T2 floors as the certificate (direct lower bounds independent of any independence model), (b) report a *bounded-bias* proxy for tightening under a stated positive-correlation model, handling the $\Pr(\text{disagree})>0.5$ regime explicitly rather than taking an imaginary square root, and (c) present the whole of T3 only *with* empirical validation on labelled RNP.

*Exact assumptions.* A bounded positive-correlation / partial-calibration model of member errors given the stratum, assumptions known to fail under this shift. Its value is quantifying the achievable tightening and directly measuring the co-folding failure-correlation gap, itself a publishable empirical finding, never a guarantee.

#### Method & experiments

**Assets and the single-dataset validation reality.** RNP (~2,073 complexes, released predictions for AF3, Boltz-1/2, Chai-1, Protenix, RFAA, plus ground-truth ligand-RMSD and pre-computed Tanimoto/pocket/temporal novelty) is the **only** dataset that ships *both* raw per-model predicted ligand coordinates *and* crystal poses, so tightness and validity of the metric certificate are checkable only there. This is stated plainly: **metric-certificate validation is single-dataset (RNP).** **FoldBench is scalar-only** (its released `foldbench_protein_ligand_confidence_rmsd.csv` carries `ranking_score` + `lrmsd` but no per-model predicted ligand coordinates, confirmed in `io/foldbench.py`), so a FoldBench pose-disagreement certificate would require re-running $K$ models on ~558 targets (heavy GPU, violates reuse-first); it is **out of scope for v1**, contributing only the scalar-confidence agreement transfer. **Mac1 557** (jongbin99/Cofolding) ships multi-model poses but crystal coords are release-delayed, so it yields an **unvalidated deployment demonstration only** (label-free floor computed, no floor-vs-truth check), not corroborating evidence. The generalization narrative therefore leans on the RNP temporal split plus per-stratum breadth, and states explicitly that this is the ceiling until Mac1 coords release. PoseBusters V1/V2 sanity-checks the $\mathrm{RMSD}\le2\,\text{Å}$ label.

1. **Single-frame pose extraction and RMSD, label recomputed in the same frame (real task, budgeted ~3-4 days).** The existing `cross_model_pose_features` (`pose_agreement.py:238`) superposes each model onto the *reference model's* pocket **per pair**, so its pairwise distances live in different frames and do **not** satisfy the triangle inequality T1 needs. D1 replaces this with a single fixed frame: superpose every model's *protein* onto **one** reference receptor exactly once (the crystal frame for validation; $m_0$'s receptor for deployment), then compute symmetry-corrected ligand RMSD with `spyrmsd(..., minimize=False)` in that frozen frame. **Crucially, the label $\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,y^\star)$ is recomputed in this identical single reference frame with the identical `spyrmsd` settings, not taken from RNP's shipped BiSyRMSD** (whose alignment convention differs, so the triangle inequality would not connect it to the pairwise disagreement RMSD, and the floor-vs-truth check would test the wrong quantity). The reference-frame choice is logged a priori, and the inter-model pocket-superposition residual $\varepsilon$ is recorded per target to instantiate the $2\varepsilon$ deployment slack.
2. **Accept region.** Reuse the existing `conformal`/`selective` stack (crepes, split/Mondrian) to define $A=\{s\ge\tau_c\}$ per novelty stratum for each deployed model, and to produce the per-model $R_{\mathrm{ref},m}(\tau_c)$ from the weighted-CP baseline; average into $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$.
3. **Floor + certificate.** Compute the T1 pairwise and packing floors ($\alpha(G)$ via networkx, $K\le6$ so graphs are tiny), then apply the correct finite-sample tool to each object: Clopper-Pearson LCB for the pre-registered-pair and any-pair Bernoulli statistics (with the $1/2$ and $1/K$ constants respectively), and a bounded-mean UCB (empirical-Bernstein / Hoeffding-Bentkus from the RCPS stack) for the packing estimand $\mathbb E[\alpha(G)\mid A]$. Report the certified lower bound on $\bar R$ and $R_{\max}$ and the certified $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}$ with $(1-\delta)$ coverage.
4. **Validation harness (single-frame, same-metric).** Because RNP supplies crystal poses, measure with labels recomputed in the same frame as step 1: (i) tightness, certified floor vs realized $\bar R$ / $R_{\max}$ per stratum; (ii) the T3 correlation gap, observed disagreement vs the independence prediction; (iii) the vacuity check, distribution of single-frame pairwise pose-RMSD among accepted complexes and how often it exceeds $2\rho$ (and $2\rho+2\varepsilon$).
5. **Stratification.** Repeat conditional on the novelty axis (max ligand Tanimoto bins, pocket similarity/TM-score, temporal cutoff), because marginal validity hides per-stratum danger.

**SOTA baseline to beat and metric.** The baseline is native-confidence thresholding plus the covariate-weighted certificate $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$ (the project's E1-E3 stack, and the Hou-group AF3/Boltz/Protenix ipTM-as-VS-signal line, Chem Sci D5SC06481C, which documents novelty degradation but supplies *no* guarantee and *no* abstention). The headline metric is **coverage of the ensemble concept gap**: the fraction of (stratum) cells where the certified lower bound on $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}$ is (a) valid (does not exceed the realized $\bar\Delta_c^{\mathrm{ens}}$ on labelled RNP, at level $\delta$) and (b) strictly positive, i.e. detects under-reporting the baseline certificate misses. Because the accept region is where confidence is high and confidence correlates with pose agreement, the certified-positive-gap headline is *not* assumed for the deployed $A$; the metric is reported across the **full threshold sweep**, not only at $\tau_c$, so a showable valid result exists even if $A$ itself is agreement-saturated. Secondary: floor tightness (certified vs realized $\bar R$), and the measured failure-correlation gap from T3.

FoldBench pose-disagreement is **out of scope for v1** (needs coordinate regeneration); FoldBench contributes only the scalar-confidence agreement transfer.
- **Weeks 11-14.** Writeup, honest scope statement (the 2507.00057 and 2603.14070 concessions, the ensemble-object framing, and the single-dataset validation ceiling), novelty defense, and figures. Target: MoML 2026 short paper (Sept 1) and the arXiv preprint, feeding the archival journal paper.

#### Risks & mitigations

- **The bare pairwise floor and its factor one-half are prior art.** Both arXiv:2507.00057 (discrete I/O) and arXiv:2603.14070 (classification shift, exact $\tfrac12\eta^\star$ minimax bound) publish it. Mitigation: concede both up front and rest novelty only on the metric conversion to a latent target, the packing floor, the T2b decomposition, and the Theorem-1 reconciliation; drop any language implying the pairwise floor or its constant is novel.
- **Per-model blame is impossible; certify the ensemble.** Disagreement cannot say *which* model erred, so a per-$m_0$ floor is invalid (and provably label-free-impossible, T2c). Mitigation: certify $\bar R$ / $R_{\max}$ and define the concept gap against $\bar R_{\mathrm{ref}}$; re-derive the Theorem-1 reconciliation for the ensemble object.
- **Wrong constant on the max-over-pairs statistic.** $R_{\max}\ge\tfrac12 p$ is false for the any-pair disagreement rate. Mitigation: use $\tfrac12$ only for a single pre-registered pair; use the union-bound $\tfrac1K$ for the any-pair statistic; lead with the exact packing floor $\bar R\ge1-\mathbb E[\alpha(G)]/K$; Bonferroni-split $\delta$ if maxing over all pairs.
- **Wrong finite-sample tool.** Clopper-Pearson is binomial-only. Mitigation: Clopper-Pearson for the binary pair / union statistics; a bounded-mean UCB (empirical-Bernstein / Hoeffding-Bentkus) for the packing estimand $\mathbb E[\alpha(G)]$.
- **Deployment frame is not the crystal frame.** The triangle inequality bridges geometric disagreement to the crystal-referenced label only in a frame containing $y^\star$; at deployment disagreement lives in $m_0$'s frame. This is a validity matter, not logging. Mitigation: measure the inter-model pocket-superposition residual $\varepsilon$, trigger only above $2\rho+2\varepsilon$, and subtract the slack so the deployment bound stays one-sided-valid; report the $\varepsilon$ distribution.
- **Label-source mismatch in validation.** Reusing RNP's shipped BiSyRMSD as the label while computing disagreement with a new single-frame `spyrmsd` breaks the triangle-inequality link. Mitigation: recompute $\mathrm{RMSD}(x_m,y^\star)$ in the identical reference frame and `spyrmsd` settings used for disagreement.
- **Accept-vs-agreement coupling and vacuity.** The accept region is high-confidence, hence high-agreement, so the floor is systematically weakest exactly on $A$, and the consensus regime (T2c) reads $\approx0$ where risk is highest. Mitigation: measure the accept-and-novelty-conditioned disagreement in Weeks 1-2 before committing the money figure; pre-register the theory-first framing as primary; report the floor across the full threshold sweep, not only $A$.
- **Sample-size erosion in the interesting cells.** ~12% multi-ligand systems drop to NaN (`pose_agreement.py:25-26`), cross-model pairs are skipped on element-multiset mismatch (`line 236`, bites on protonation/explicit-H differences), and high-novelty strata are already thin, so per-stratum $n_g$ can collapse to single digits and the LCB reads $\approx0$ for pure sample-size reasons. Mitigation: report effective $n$ per (stratum $\cap$ accept $\cap$ valid graph) and the pair-skip rate in Weeks 1-2; use a **heavy-atom-only element-multiset key** (H already stripped in `_heavy`) to recover pairs currently dropped over protonation; pool adjacent strata or widen $\delta$ where thin; declare non-certifiable cells up front.
- **Single-dataset validation.** Only RNP ships both coordinates and crystal labels. Mitigation: state the ceiling plainly; present Mac1 as an unvalidated deployment demo, the temporal split as a split not a dataset, and FoldBench pose-disagreement as out of scope until coords regenerate.
- **T3 assumptions fail under shift.** Independence/calibration break precisely under novel-chemotype shift. Mitigation: present T3 strictly as a validated-on-labels heuristic with bounded bias, never the closed-form inversion, and use the correlation gap as an empirical finding.

#### Novelty defense

- *Incoherence as an Oracle-less Measure of Error in LLM-Based Code Generation* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.00057 (AAAI 2026). **Closest prior on the core mechanism (discrete I/O).** Formalizes the pointwise incoherence inequality (disagreement floors failure probability, sound, never false positives). D1 concedes the sound-lower-bound idea to it and differentiates on metric geometry of a continuous structured output referencing a latent target.
- *Structured Credal Learning* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14070 (2026). **Closest prior on the factor.** Theorem 5.5 proves a $\tfrac12\eta^\star$ minimax lower bound where $\eta^\star$ is the maximum pairwise disagreement rate, in a distribution-shift / labeling-uncertainty regime (credal-set diameter equals maximum pairwise disagreement). Classification / TV only, discrete labels, no continuous-metric output, no $2\rho$ trigger to a latent crystal pose. D1 concedes the factor one-half to it and rests novelty on the metric conversion, the packing floor, the decomposition, and the Theorem-1 reconciliation.
- *Cross-Model Disagreement as a Label-Free Correctness Signal* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25450 (2026). LLM-only empirical heuristic; no finite-sample bound, no metric geometry.
- *(Almost) Provable Error Bounds Under Distribution Shift via Disagreement Discrepancy (Dis²)* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.00312 (2023). *Upper* bound via a trained critic and source labels; D1 is a training-free, assumption-light *lower* bound on the irreducible gap.
- *Assessing Generalization of SGD via Disagreement (GDE)* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.13799 (2021). Two-sided estimate under calibration that fails under this shift; used only as a caveated tightening.
- *The Limits of Distribution-Free Conditional Predictive Inference* — https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.04684 (2021). Foundational impossibility Theorem 1 specializes; D1 respects it by certifying danger (a lower bound), never conditional safety.
- *Conformal Prediction Under Covariate Shift (weighted conformal)* — https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.06019 (2019). - *Consensus Docking* — https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ci300399w (2013). Empirical origin of "pose agreement filters wrong poses" (55-64% → 82%+); classical docking, no guarantee, no finite-sample certificate, no shift/concept-gap framing, the folk wisdom D1 certifies and decomposes.

## 2. D2 -- Label-efficient certification under concept shift (query complexity)

**Setup and notation (carried from the project theorem).** Fix a novelty stratum `g` (a bin of the training-set-similarity axis: max ligand Tanimoto, pocket TM-score / SuCOS, temporal cutoff). The deployed rule is `accept iff s >= tau_c`, where `s` is the frozen co-folding score (ipTM, `ranking_score`, chain-pair ipTM, or a calibration-only combiner) and the threshold `tau_c` is **fixed and given** (it is the deployed operating point, not a boundary to be learned). The per-pose loss is the binding-mode-error indicator `L = 1{ligand-RMSD > 2A} in [0,1]`. Write `R_ref,g(tau_c)` for the *reference* (source-calibrated) selective risk on the accept region of stratum `g`, `Delta_bar_g` for the accept-region **concept gap** of Thm 1 (the label-free-invariant term, `Delta_bar_g = E_Q[L | accept, g] - E_ref[L | accept, g]`), so the realized target selective risk is `R_Q,g(tau_c) = R_ref,g(tau_c) + Delta_bar_g`. Let `alpha` be the target risk level, `delta` the failure probability, `n_g` the number of *target-stratum* labels queried, and define the **certification margin**

```
m_g := alpha - R_ref,g(tau_c) - Delta_bar_g = alpha - R_Q,g(tau_c).
```

The second equality is definitional and is load-bearing. Because `tau_c` is fixed, certifying `R_Q,g(tau_c) <= alpha` is exactly the one-sided test of whether the accepted-region Bernoulli mean `R_Q,g = E_Q[L | accept, g]` lies below `alpha`. This is **fixed-mean estimation**, not active learning of a decision surface. A consequence that the audit makes explicit and that this revision now states up front: the certification procedure observes only `R_Q,g` through queried labels; it never observes or uses `R_ref,g` or `Delta_bar_g`, so the achievable label count is governed by the **true target margin** `alpha - R_Q,g` and nothing else. The `R_ref + Delta_bar` decomposition of that margin is an explanatory device for *where the impossibility bites*, not a quantity the estimator exploits.

The question D2 answers: given the Thm 1 floor, how many target-stratum labels `n_g` are necessary and sufficient to *certify* `R_Q,g(tau_c) <= alpha` at confidence `1 - delta`, which stratum-level regime makes any label-free certificate impossible, and whether a cheap label-free co-folding covariate measurably reduces the labels needed on the strata where certification is feasible.

#### What changed after audit (scope corrections up front)

Six corrections reshape this section. They are stated here so no later claim rests on a retracted mechanism, and so the direction's honest deliverable is visible before the targets.

1. **Target 1 is prediction-powered risk control, off the shelf, and is not a novelty claim.** The variance reduction `sigma_g^2 -> sigma_g^2(1 - rho_g^2)` via a label-free covariate is precisely prediction-powered inference (PPI): PPI is a control-variate variance reduction whose minimum variance is `(1 - rho^2)` times the label-only baseline. The mechanism is the published named method, not a fresh estimator. Target 1 is therefore prediction-powered risk control (Angelopoulos and Zrnic 2023; semi-supervised risk control via PPI, arXiv:2412.11174; active multiple-PPI, arXiv:2605.08429) instantiated on the accepted-pose ratio risk. It is the reliable deliverable and is never presented as the contribution.

2. **The concept-gap-keyed margin is not a new rate; the one differentiated theoretical morsel is the domain-grounded ambiguity ball.** Since the procedure depends only on `alpha - R_Q,g`, the achievability count is the bare-margin rate with the *target* margin, which is exactly the rate already proved by Conformal Selective Acting (CSA, arXiv:2605.20270, Thm 5.4). The `m_g = alpha - R_ref - Delta_bar` framing is expository. The single element that is genuinely new is a **DRO ambiguity ball whose radius is calibrated to the measured co-folding feature-conditional Bayes noise on RNP** (a domain-grounded ambiguity set rather than a generic one). Wasserstein-regularized CP (arXiv:2501.13430) and pseudo-calibrated CP under shift (arXiv:2602.14913) already decompose coverage gap into covariate and concept-shift contributions; they do coverage-gap decomposition, not label query complexity, and neither ties the ambiguity radius to a measured domain noise floor.

3. **The estimator is PPI / a PPI-style confidence sequence, not a WSR bet on an unbounded variable.** The WSR empirical-Bernstein confidence sequence is defined for variables in a bounded interval, and the control-variate variable `L - beta(c - E[c])` is unbounded, so it cannot be plugged into a WSR bet while retaining the empirical-Bernstein half-width. The certificate is therefore built on prediction-powered inference / sequential PPI++ (which is exactly anytime-valid variance reduction from a label-free predictor) for the control-variate estimand, and on a plain bounded WSR confidence sequence for the label-only estimand. The `beta` coefficient is fixed on a held-out split (or pre-registered), never estimated on the same scarce labels it corrects. The `(1 - rho^2)` gain is stated as an asymptotic upper limit that is not realized at very small `n_g`.

4. **The query unit is the target, not the (target, model) pose.** RNP ships 4-5 predictions of the same complex (about 13,535 rows over about 2,073 systems), whose losses are strongly clustered by target (a hard pocket makes the models fail together). Pooling those as independent Bernoulli draws makes the confidence sequence anti-conservative and its label budget optimistic, and it silently changes the estimand to a 5-model mixture risk that certifies no single deployed model. The certification population and budget are therefore defined at the **target level**: query one pose per target (the deployed / best-ranked model), and report every budget in **independent target-labels**. A finite-population, within-target-clustered variant is available as a validity check, but the headline is per-model, per-target.

5. **The control-variate saving is measured, small on feasible strata, and folded into the empirical story rather than sold as a speedup.** A pilot measurement (below) gives `|rho_g| ~ 0.22-0.25` on the low-novelty feasible strata, i.e. variance kept `(1 - rho_g^2) ~ 0.94-0.95`, roughly a 5% saving that sits visually on top of the plain confidence sequence at the tiny budgets in play. The robust, large win over passive Hoeffding-RCPS is **empirical-Bernstein variance-adaptivity for small-`p` Bernoulli** (accepted-pose error rate `p ~ 0.08-0.18` on feasible strata), which is real and does not depend on any covariate. The control variate is presented as a measured, mostly-null add-on.

6. **The disagreement-collapse prediction is contradicted by the data and is downgraded to a measurement, not a predicted theorem.** The earlier narrative predicted `rho_g -> 0` on high-novelty strata. The pilot shows the opposite direction: `rho_g` is *larger* on the infeasible high-novelty bins (`~0.43` via pose-RMSD spread) than on the feasible low-novelty bins (`~0.22`). The honest finding is therefore not a collapse but a **feasibility mismatch**: the covariate carries the most error signal exactly where `m_g < 0` makes certification impossible, and carries little where certification is feasible. Target 3 is rewritten around this measured profile and makes no confident collapse claim.

#### Problem and why the open part is narrow

The impossibility side of the project theorem says a label-free certificate silently under-reports realized risk by `Delta_bar_g`, so certification in a shifted stratum requires spending target labels. The bare label-query-complexity of that certification, at a fixed threshold on a safe Bernoulli instance, is settled: CSA Thm 5.4 gives the rate-optimal `Theta(margin^{-2} log(1/delta))`. What remains open, and what this direction can honestly own, is narrow and domain-specific:

- a per-stratum **feasibility map** for co-folding pose certification on RNP (where `m_g > 0` and target labels certify, versus where `m_g <= 0` and no label-free certificate can, which is the impossibility regime the project theorem predicts);
- a **domain-grounded ambiguity ball** whose radius is fixed by the measured feature-conditional Bayes noise of co-folding pose error, which scopes exactly how much room a concept-shift adversary has;
- a **measurement** of whether cross-model / ensemble co-folding disagreement is a usable PPI control covariate along the training-similarity axis, reported with its feasibility mismatch.

The scaffolding on the four sides is fixed and cited as such:

- **Active RCPS** (Csillag et al., arXiv:2406.10490, NeurIPS 2024) does budgeted label acquisition for risk control in the i.i.d. case with a wealth / e-process, assumes exchangeability, and proves no label lower bound. It is the upper-bound template.
- **Prediction-powered inference** (Angelopoulos and Zrnic 2023, arXiv:2301.09633; semi-supervised risk control arXiv:2412.11174; active multiple-PPI arXiv:2605.08429) is the exact `(1 - rho^2)` control-variate mechanism of Target 1.
- **Conformal Selective Acting** (arXiv:2605.20270, Thm 5.4) proves the fixed-threshold Bernoulli certificate rate `Theta(margin^{-2} log(1/delta))`. This is the precedent Target 2 specializes; the bare rate is not claimed as novel.
- **CRC certification impossibility** (Gupta et al., arXiv:2606.29054) proves a distribution-free abstention-fraction floor with no target labels. It asks no label-complexity question; the D2 question is the dual.
- **Coverage-gap decomposition under shift** (Wasserstein-regularized CP arXiv:2501.13430; pseudo-calibrated CP arXiv:2602.14913) separates covariate from concept-shift gap contributions but does not ask label query complexity.
- **Lower-bound machinery** is classical: Le Cam / Fano two-point reductions, and the multicalibration sample-complexity separation (arXiv:2604.21923).

The honest read, which must survive an interview: the upper bound is prediction-powered risk control fused with active RCPS and Thm 3, off the shelf. The bare lower bound is CSA Thm 5.4. The genuinely new content is the domain-grounded ambiguity ball, the per-stratum feasibility map on real co-folding data, and the measured disagreement-error profile with its feasibility mismatch. The direction is carried by the empirical certification study, not by a headline theorem.

#### Target theorem(s)

**Target 1 -- Upper bound / achievability (the reliable deliverable, prediction-powered risk control off the shelf).**
There is a certification procedure that, using `n_g` independent target-labels drawn under a fixed, label-free sampling design from stratum `g` at the target level, certifies `R_Q,g(tau_c) <= alpha` with probability `>= 1 - delta` whenever `m_g > 0`, with label budget

```
n_g = O( sigma_g^2 (1 - rho_g^2) log(1/delta) / m_g^2 ),
```

where `sigma_g^2` is the in-stratum variance of the accepted-pose loss `L | accept, g`, `m_g = alpha - R_Q,g(tau_c)` is the true target margin, and `rho_g = corr(L, c | accept, g)` for a label-free control covariate `c`. The `(1 - rho_g^2)` factor is the PPI control-variate variance reduction, stated as an asymptotic upper limit.

- *Provenance.* This is prediction-powered risk control (arXiv:2412.11174, arXiv:2605.08429; PPI origin arXiv:2301.09633) instantiated on the accepted-pose ratio risk, with the target margin substituted for `alpha`. It adds no new estimator and is not a novelty claim.
- *Proof strategy.* Estimate `E_Q[L | accept, g]` with an anytime-valid confidence sequence and fire certification when the CI upper edge drops below `alpha`, i.e. when the half-width falls below `m_g`. Use a bounded empirical-Bernstein (WSR, arXiv:2010.09686) confidence sequence on `L in [0,1]` for the label-only estimator (its variance-adaptive half-width `O(sigma_g sqrt(log(1/delta)/n_g))` is the concrete saving over Hoeffding-based passive RCPS at small `p`). Layer the label-free covariate through prediction-powered / sequential PPI++ rather than through a WSR bet on the unbounded adjusted variable, with `beta` fixed on a held-out split. Insert `Delta_bar_g` from Thm 1 only to explain why the margin, not `alpha`, drives the budget.
- *Sampling-design validity.* The estimator must stay unbiased for `E_Q[L | accept, g]`. Primary design: uniform-within-stratum sampling at the target level, under which the confidence sequence is directly valid. A non-uniform label-free design (oversampling high-disagreement targets) is admissible only paired with a design-aware / importance-weighted confidence sequence using the known weights, and is included solely to confirm it does not beat uniform for a fixed mean. Naive disagreement-ranked querying with an unweighted mean is invalid and is not used.
- *Assumptions.* Bounded loss; label-free sampling design with known weights; strata fixed a priori and non-degenerate; accept probability bounded away from 0; one pose per target so draws are independent (or an explicit clustered variant).
- *Where it fails.* The margin `m_g` is negative in exactly the high-novelty strata that motivate the project, so the theorem is vacuous there and is stated only on `{g : m_g > 0}`. The `(1 - rho_g^2)` gain is empirically about 5% on the feasible strata (see pilot), so it is reported as a measured add-on, and the certificate's value rests on empirical-Bernstein variance-adaptivity plus the honest feasibility map.

**Target 2 -- Reduced minimax lower bound and a domain-grounded ambiguity ball (scoped hard).**
The bare fixed-`delta` two-point bound is a re-derivation of CSA Thm 5.4 and is cited as such. The scoped deliverable is the reduced construction plus the domain-grounded ball.

*Reduced two-point bound (the Weeks 9-12 deliverable).* Any certificate built from covariate-measurable / label-free signals plus `n` target-stratum labels that is (a) *valid* (when it fires, `R_Q,g <= alpha` w.p. `>= 1 - delta`) and (b) *complete* (it fires on every feasible instance with margin `>= m` w.p. `>= 1 - delta`) must, in the worst case over a pair `Q_0, Q_1` that match the covariate marginal and the two logged disagreement summaries (`ens_ranking_std`, `xmodel_iptm_std`), use

```
n = Omega( sigma_L^2 / m^2 )   at constant delta,
```

where `sigma_L^2 = R_Q(1 - R_Q)` is the accept-region **marginal loss** Bernoulli variance. The novel content over CSA is only that the two matched moments carry the blindness (the information content of the Thm 1 tower identity: a covariate weight recovers only the source conditional `eta_P`).

*Domain-grounded ambiguity ball (the one differentiated morsel; partly future work).* Because `L = 1{RMSD > 2A}` is deterministic given the true structure, a nontrivial concept-shift adversary exists only to the extent the observable features do not determine `L`. Fit `eta(x) = P(L = 1 | features)` on RNP, measure the residual feature-conditional Bayes noise `nu(x)`, and size an f-divergence / CVaR ball by `nu`. The lower bound then holds on `{m : m <= m_max(nu)}`, where `m_max` is the largest mean separation the noise budget admits, given explicitly as a function of the fitted-`eta` residual variance. Report the empirical `m_max` on RNP so the reader sees the range where the bound bites, and report where `nu ~ 0` makes the ball near-vacuous.

- *Distinct-variance note (audit fix).* The Le Cam term uses the accept-region marginal loss variance `sigma_L^2 = R_Q(1 - R_Q)` (bounded away from 0 even when features nearly determine `L`), whereas the ball is sized by the feature-conditional Bayes noise `nu` (which can be near 0). These are different objects and carry different symbols. The achievable separation `2m` is itself capped by `nu`, so `m` is not a free knob; the bound is non-vacuous only for `m <= m_max(nu)`.
- *Completeness condition.* Validity alone is met by the trivial never-certify rule, so the bound is a power statement requiring clause (b); only then does the two-point reduction bite.
- *`log(1/delta)` scope.* A two-point Le Cam reduction yields `Omega(sigma_L^2 / m^2)` at fixed `delta` and does not produce a `log(1/delta)` factor; that factor sits only on the Target 1 upper side. The claim is "minimax-optimal up to `log(1/delta)` and constants."
- *Explicitly out of the timeline.* The general f-divergence / CVaR ball that matches *all* observable confidence statistics jointly is genuine measure-theoretic research and is stated as future work, not a Weeks 9-12 deliverable. The scoped result matches the covariate marginal plus the two logged summaries only.
- *Where it fails.* If `nu ~ 0` across feasible strata, the ball is thin and the hardness claim is weak; this is reported, not assumed. The paper's contribution does not depend on this lower bound closing.

**Target 3 -- Disagreement as a PPI control covariate: measured profile and feasibility mismatch (the empirical crux, no predicted collapse).**
This is domain-specific measurement, not a new theoretical finding and not a predicted collapse. Prior work (disagreement-discrepancy Bayes-inconsistency, arXiv:2512.05931; correlated errors in large models, arXiv:2506.07962) establishes that model-disagreement surrogates decouple from error when predictors are positively correlated. We measure, for cross-model and ensemble co-folding pose error along the training-similarity axis, the PPI control-variate correlation `rho_g = corr(L, c | accept, g)` and the realized saving `1 - rho_g^2`.

- *Measured profile (pilot, revealed on RNP where all labels exist).* On the feasible low/medium-novelty strata `|rho_g| ~ 0.22-0.25` (scores-only best covariate), so `1 - rho_g^2 ~ 0.94-0.95`, a roughly 5% saving. On the infeasible high-novelty bins `|rho_g| ~ 0.43` via pose-RMSD spread, i.e. *larger*, not collapsing. The result is therefore a **feasibility mismatch**, not a collapse: the covariate carries the most error signal exactly where `m_g < 0` makes certification impossible, and carries little where certification is feasible. This is the honest finding and it is directly opposite to the earlier `rho_g -> 0` narrative, which the data does not support.
- *Content.* There is no positive theorem claiming an active speedup and no claimed collapse. The result is the measured `rho_g` profile across strata, its small feasible-side saving, and its feasibility mismatch, tied to the sign of `m_g`. A documented mismatch is a publishable finding.
- *Covariate selection (audit fix).* Because raw ipTM is known-miscalibrated and a weak feature, the analysis compares candidate covariates rather than staking the story on one: scores-only `ens_ranking_std` and `xmodel_iptm_std` (reproducible without the coordinate tarball), intra-model 5-sample ensemble spread, and PoseBusters strain / PB-validity. Report whichever shows `|rho|` bounded away from 0; if none does on feasible strata, the value proposition falls back entirely on empirical-Bernstein variance-adaptivity.
- *Assumptions.* Disagreement is at least partially predictive of accepted-pose error on some feasible stratum; models are approximately conditionally exchangeable given covariates for the correlation analysis.
- *Where it fails.* If `|rho_g|` is near 0 on every feasible stratum, the PPI add-on vanishes there and the certificate reduces to plain WSR-beats-Hoeffding, which is stated plainly and is still a valid deliverable.

#### Method and experiments

Everything runs on assets already in scope. RNP has all RMSD labels, so query policies are benchmarked by *revealing* labels only when queried; the only real cost is per-stratum bookkeeping.

0. **Feasibility-and-count gate (run first, at the target level).** For a candidate binning, tabulate accepted **target** count per bin (one pose per target, the deployed / best-ranked model) against the predicted `n_g(m_g) = O(sigma_g^2 (1 - rho_g^2) log(1/delta) / m_g^2)` at `alpha in {0.1, 0.2}`, `delta = 0.1`. Do **not** pool the 4-5 per-target model rows as independent draws; report the budget in independent target-labels. Choose bin granularity (coarser bins, or a data-driven feasible / infeasible dichotomy) so that at least 2-3 bins are provably certifiable (feasible `m_g > 0` and enough independent target-labels to reach `n_g`), preferring `alpha = 0.2` where margins are wider. If per-model, per-target counts are too thin, report "insufficient data to certify here" rather than recovering counts by pooling. Every later claim is gated on this table.
1. **Strata, margins, and Bayes noise.** Bin RNP by the novelty axis. For each stratum estimate `R_ref,g(tau_c)`, `Delta_bar_g`, `m_g` from the full labels; fit `eta(x) = P(L = 1 | features)`, record the residual feature-conditional Bayes noise `nu`, and derive the empirical `m_max(nu)` that scopes Target 2's ball. Partition strata into feasible (`m_g > 0`) and infeasible (`m_g <= 0`). Report this first; it delineates where any certification claim is meaningful and where the impossibility regime lives.
2. **Simulated label-reveal harness (target-level).** Query loop that hides RMSD labels and reveals one target-label when a policy requests it. Estimators: a bounded WSR empirical-Bernstein confidence sequence on `E_Q[L | accept, g]` (label-only), and a prediction-powered / PPI++ confidence sequence with the label-free covariate and held-out `beta`. Designs: (a) uniform-within-stratum passive baseline (Hoeffding-RCPS and WSR-uniform), (b) uniform sampling + PPI covariate (Target 1), (c) non-uniform disagreement-oversampling with a design-aware / importance-weighted confidence sequence, included only to confirm it does not beat (b) for a fixed mean. Record independent target-labels-to-certify per stratum.
3. **Certification-vs-budget curves.** For each estimator and stratum, plot certification success rate against `n_g` (target-labels), with the Target 1 prediction overlaid. Attribute the win over passive Hoeffding-RCPS to empirical-Bernstein variance-adaptivity at small `p`, and present the PPI covariate as a measured, mostly-null add-on, not label placement.
4. **Disagreement-as-covariate measurement (the crux).** Measure `rho_g` and the realized `1 - rho_g^2` per stratum for the candidate covariates, scores-only in the primary analysis. Report the measured profile and the feasibility mismatch (small saving on feasible strata, larger `|rho|` on infeasible strata where `m_g < 0`). Do not assert a collapse.
5. **Cross-dataset and prospective checks (gated on data verification).** *Before Week 6, verify FoldBench redistributes released per-prediction confidences plus ligand-RMSD labels per model the way RNP does.* If yes, replay the certificate on the low-homology / `< 0.50` Tanimoto "unseen ligand" subset. ## 3. D3 -- Continuous-novelty localized conformal (footnote extension)

#### Problem & why it is open

The discrete repair in E3 partitions predictions into Mondrian novelty strata (Tanimoto bins, pocket-similarity bins, temporal cutoff) and recalibrates within each bin. Binning is arbitrary at the edges and wastes data: a prediction just inside a bin boundary is calibrated against a neighbor further away than a prediction just outside it. The natural fix is to replace the discrete stratifier with a continuous novelty coordinate `v = novelty(X)` and localize the calibration with a kernel. This is where honesty requires splitting the direction into two layers.

The *method* layer is settled prior art. Guan's Localized Conformal Prediction (Biometrika 2023, arXiv:2106.08460) already kernel-reweights calibration nonconformity scores by covariate proximity to the test point, keeps an assumption-free finite-sample marginal guarantee, and gives local coverage under smoothness, with a bandwidth `h` that interpolates from split conformal (large `h`) to fully local (small `h`). Hore & Barber's Randomly-Localized CP (RLCP, arXiv:2310.07850, JRSSB 2024) adds randomization so the interval has a provable *approximate* conditional-coverage guarantee that explicitly respects the Barber et al. pointwise-impossibility result and stays valid under covariate shift. CoDrug (arXiv:2310.12033, NeurIPS 2023) is the closest domain neighbor: KDE / density-ratio-weighted conformal keyed on a molecular-novelty axis, correcting covariate shift for a scalar drug property. SpeedCP (arXiv:2509.24100, 2025) and Online Localized CP (arXiv:2605.05497, 2026) supply adaptive and online bandwidth machinery. Swapping our discrete strata for a kernel on `v` is a textbook application of these papers. We do not claim it as new methodology, and framing it that way would be scooped and indefensible in an interview.

The *contribution* layer is narrower than a first reading of the discrete theorem suggests, and we scope it precisely. A reweighting-invariant selective-risk certification floor is already published: arXiv:2606.29054 (When Can Conformal Risk Control Certify LLM Outputs?, June 2026), Proposition 3, proves that any distribution-free selective predictor certified at level `alpha` with probability `>= 1 - delta` must abstain on at least `(mu - alpha)/(1 - alpha)` of inputs, a floor that is "invariant to score quality and bound tightness" and that "no method escapes through better calibration or reweighting alone." That is the same qualitative phenomenon our core Thm 1 captures. So the concept-floor idea is not first-of-kind and we must not present it as such. The 2606.29054 floor is a single *global* number keyed to base risk `mu` versus `alpha`, with no covariate structure, no localization, and no target-label achievability. Our surviving delta is the *covariate-localized* refinement: (i) promote the discrete accept-region gap `Delta_bar_c` to a per-novelty-level function `Delta_bar(v)` tied to `(eta_Q - eta_P)` along the label-free novelty axis, (ii) show that any label-free kernel localization (Guan / RLCP / SpeedCP / CoDrug-style density-ratio reweighting), at a *fixed* kernel, cannot certify realized selective risk below a local concept floor no matter what covariate reweighting it applies, and (iii) give a kernel-local achievability companion, honestly scoped to the novelty range where target labels exist. The localized-CP literature gives the estimator and is silent on a covariate-localized, per-novelty-level selective-risk concept floor, on target-label achievability under concept shift, and on any co-folding instantiation. That is a genuine but modest footnote-level delta, which is exactly the intended scope.

**Minimum-viable novel core (not cuttable).** To keep the direction defensible even if later stages stall, we fix a non-cuttable core up front: (a) T1, the per-novelty covariate-localized concept floor `Delta_bar_h(v)` tied to `(eta_Q - eta_P)`; (b) the box-kernel achievability floor-matching statement of T2, specifically the uniform-weight `m_h` reduction, which is the one genuinely new technical step that separates this from a fixed-kernel corollary of Thm 1; and (c) the empirical characterization of `Delta_bar(v)` on RNP over the powered novelty range. The box-kernel achievability proposition is the load-bearing novelty. Only the Gaussian-kernel empirical curves and the adaptive / online bandwidth machinery (SpeedCP, Online-LCP) are cuttable. We cite Conditional Conformal Risk Adaptation (arXiv:2504.07611, 2025) as the nearest conditional-CRC achievability machinery and state the differentiator plainly: our achievability side is a per-novelty *concept-gap* floor plus an explicit achievability price in *accepted* target labels `m_h`, not a re-derivation of stratified / group-conditional CRC calibration.

#### Target theorem(s)

Notation builds on the project theorem. `X` is the input (target-ligand complex), `s = score(X)` the frozen covariate-measurable confidence (ipTM, ranking_score, or a calibration-only combiner), `v = novelty(X)` a covariate-measurable label-free novelty coordinate (for example max ligand Tanimoto to train). The deployed rule accepts iff `s >= tau`. `eta_P(x) = P_source(err | x)` is the source error conditional and `eta_Q(x) = P_target(err | x)` the target one; the loss is the binding-mode error `1{ligand-RMSD > 2 A}`. `R_ref(tau)` is the reference (source-calibrated) accept-region selective risk, `R_Q(tau)` the realized target selective risk. In the discrete theorem, per stratum `c`, `R_Q(tau_c) = R_ref(tau_c) + Delta_bar_c` with `Delta_bar_c` the accept-region concept gap invariant to label-free weights, and Thm 3 gives in-stratum achievability with `n_g` target labels and slack `1/(n_g+1)` for coverage plus `O(sqrt(log(1/delta)/n_g))` for the ratio selective risk.

**Target T1 (continuous-novelty impossibility; footnote corollary of Thm 1).** Fix a localization kernel `K_h(v, v')` whose weights are functions of covariates only, and evaluate both risks under the *same* target-covariate kernel measure. Let `E_h^Q[ . | v]` denote the `Q`-covariate kernel-weighted conditional at novelty level `v`, and define the reference certificate `R_ref^loc(tau; v)` as the oracle that plugs the source conditional `eta_P` into that same target-kernel measure. Then

```
R_Q^loc(tau; v) = R_ref^loc(tau; v) + Delta_bar_h(v),
```

where

```
Delta_bar_h(v) = E_h^Q[(eta_Q - eta_P) 1{s >= tau} | v] / E_h^Q[1{s >= tau} | v]
```

is the kernel-averaged accept-region concept gap. For a *fixed* localization kernel `K_h`, `R_ref^loc(tau; v)` and hence the certified level are invariant to *any* additional label-free covariate reweighting `w(X)`: label-free weights average `eta_P` over covariates and cannot recover `eta_Q`, so the residual `Delta_bar_h(v)` cannot be removed by reweighting. Consequently no label-free localized / kernel conformal certificate (Guan, RLCP, SpeedCP, CoDrug-style density-ratio reweighting) can certify realized selective risk below `R_ref^loc(v) + Delta_bar_h(v)`, and level `alpha` is locally unachievable wherever `Delta_bar_h(v) > alpha - R_ref^loc(v)`. The floor value `Delta_bar_h(v)` is itself kernel- and bandwidth-dependent (it is a kernel-weighted average), so we report it per chosen kernel and never claim a single kernel-free number. Invariance is stated only as "for a fixed kernel, invariant to any label-free covariate reweighting `w`," not as weights cancelling.

*Proof strategy.* Kernel-weighted lift of the tower-identity proof of Thm 1, evaluated throughout under the target-covariate kernel measure `E_h^Q[ . | v]`. Add a label-free covariate weight `w(X)`; over the accept region the weighted numerator is `E_h^Q[w (eta_Q - eta_P) 1{accept} | v]` and the denominator `E_h^Q[w 1{accept} | v]`, so `w` defines the averaging measure rather than cancelling, and no choice of `w` converts `eta_P` into `eta_Q`. One page.

*Assumptions.* `v` and `s` covariate-measurable and label-free; target conditional may differ from source (concept shift allowed); localization weights covariate-only; bandwidth `h > 0` fixed (a finite window, respecting Barber: no pointwise claim). **Sufficiency assumption (load-bearing).** For the clean concept-only identity above, both terms are integrated under one target-covariate kernel measure, which requires either that `v` is a sufficient statistic for the `P -> Q` covariate shift (conditional on `v`, the within-window covariate laws of `P` and `Q` coincide) or that the estimator itself corrects the within-window covariate law (RLCP, or an in-window density-ratio reweighting). When `v` is not sufficient, the residual the certificate leaves is `(within-window covariate-shift term) + Delta_bar_h(v)`, not the concept gap alone. `v = max ligand Tanimoto` is almost certainly *not* sufficient for a realistic source-to-target shift (target complexes differ in pocket family, protein class, ligand size, and assay), so we treat the within-window covariate term as nonzero by default and never attribute a raw box / Gaussian residual entirely to the concept gap. Two honest routes to a sharp `Delta_bar_h(v)` claim: (route A) use the RLCP-corrected measure as the headline estimator, whose in-window covariate correction removes the residual covariate term by construction; or (route B) estimate the within-window covariate term with in-window density-ratio weights and report the residual explicitly as `(covariate term + Delta_bar_h(v))`, subtracting the estimated covariate term before reporting `Delta_bar_h(v)`. The method section commits to one of these for the headline (see step 4). *Where it fails / risk:* mathematically low as a generalization, but the sufficiency assumption is the real subtlety and is stated up front; the incrementality risk over Thm 1 and over 2606.29054 is mitigated by keeping T1 a labeled corollary and framing it as the continuous-localization refinement of an already-known reweighting-invariant floor.

**Target T2 (approximate-local achievability with a novelty-adaptive bandwidth; box-kernel version is the theorem, Gaussian is empirical only).** Restrict the rigorous statement to the **box (hard-window) kernel**, where localization is literally split conformal on the window `{x : |v(x) - v| <= h}` and calibration samples inside the window carry uniform weight. Let `n_h(v)` be the target-labeled *independent systems* in the window and `m_h(v) = n_h(v) * accept_rate(v)` the *accepted* labeled target systems in the window, where `accept_rate(v) = E_h^Q[1{s >= tau} | v]`. Assume the accept-region selective-risk conditional and `Delta_bar(v)` are Holder-`beta` smooth in `v` with constant `L`, with `beta` in `(0, 1]` (Lipschitz at `beta = 1`), and `accept_rate(v)` bounded away from 0 on the window.

State the guarantee as a proposition *conditional on the realized accepted count* `m_h`. Conditioned on `m_h`, the accepted labeled subsample inside the box window carries uniform weight and the accepted-error count is a clean binomial, so a Hoeffding or empirical-Bernstein tail applies directly. Then box-kernel local recalibration attains local selective-risk level `1 - alpha` with total error bounded by

```
O(1/(m_h + 1))              [finite-sample calibration slack, on accepted labeled systems]
 + O(L * h)                 [window-width smoothness bias, interior; beta in (0,1]]
 + b_bnd(v)                 [one-sided boundary inflation, see below]
 + O(sqrt(log(1/delta)/m_h)) [ratio concentration on the uniform-weight accepted subsample, given m_h].
```

Three corrections that the earlier framing glossed. First, the `O(L h)` bias is the interior, symmetric-window rate; local averaging is 0th order, so even for smoother `Delta_bar(v)` the bias is not better than first order except in the window interior with symmetric design (the interior cap is `beta <= 2`). Second, at high novelty the window is necessarily *one-sided* (few or no labeled systems above `v`), the symmetric-moment cancellation fails, and because selective risk increases in `v` a left-only window *under*-estimates `R_Q(v)`, making the certificate anti-conservative exactly where it matters. We add an explicit one-sided conservative inflation `b_bnd(v)` (a worst-case boundary-bias term computed from an assumed upper bound on `L`) and state the boundary under-coverage plainly. Third, `L` is unverifiable and jumps at chemotype / pocket cliffs, so a usable finite-sample certificate needs a *known* upper bound on `L` to inflate conservatively. T2 is therefore a smoothness-conditional, `L`-bound-conditional oracle statement, not a distribution-free guarantee; the `L`-free route runs through T3.

The concentration and calibration terms are governed by `m_h`, not `n_h`, because selective risk is a ratio `(accepted errors)/(accepted count)`; for stability the accept rate must be bounded below, and we use a self-normalized / empirical-Bernstein or Fieller-style ratio interval for reporting when `m_h` is small. Balancing bias and variance yields, as an **informal scaling argument** rather than a constants-explicit theorem, an optimal bandwidth `h*(v) ~ (accepted-label density near v)^{-1/(2 beta + 1)}`.

*Proof strategy.* Box kernel reduces to split conformal on a hard window; conditioning on the realized `m_h` makes the accepted subsample an exchangeable uniform-weight set, so the finite-sample slack is the standard `1/(m_h + 1)` and the accepted-error count is binomial (no weighted-Hoeffding argument, because we condition on the denominator rather than treating it as random). The unconditional statement then integrates over the distribution of `m_h`, which is why the power analysis in step 2 reports the `m_h` distribution, not a point value. The window contributes the interior `O(L h)` bias plus the one-sided boundary inflation `b_bnd(v)`. The Fieller / empirical-Bernstein ratio interval is used only for reporting the band, not to establish the level. The Gaussian kernel is used empirically only, since its non-uniform weights break the clean conditional-binomial step.

*Assumptions.* Holder-`beta` (`beta` in `(0, 1]`) smoothness of the accept-region conditional and of `Delta_bar(v)`; a *known* upper bound on `L` for the boundary inflation; `accept_rate(v)` bounded away from 0; exchangeability within the box window at the `(system, method)` unit; access to `m_h` accepted labeled target systems near `v` (the achievability price). *Where it fails / risk:* moderate-to-high, stated plainly. Smoothness is unverifiable and breaks at chemotype or pocket cliffs where `Delta_bar(v)` jumps and the bias blows up. Separately, `m_h` is tiny exactly at high novelty (thin windows and low accept rate compound), so the guarantee is non-vacuous only where target labels are dense enough to pay the price; we scope it to that range (see feasibility below) rather than claim it full-range. T2's box-kernel achievability floor-matching statement is the load-bearing novelty and is *not* cut; only the Gaussian and adaptive-bandwidth extensions are cuttable.

**Target T3 (monotone-envelope local certificate; the safe, `L`-free fallback).** Two honest choices, stated separately. This is the route that removes the dependence on a known `L`.

- **(a) Marginal error envelope.** If the *marginal* error conditional `E[err | v]` is monotone nondecreasing in `v` (co-folding reliability degrades monotonically with distance to training data, an empirically testable claim), a one-sided isotonic estimator with a uniform monotone-constrained band gives `P( E_Q[err | v] <= hat_R(v) + eps(v) for all v ) >= 1 - delta`. This is the marginal error-versus-novelty curve, explicitly *not* the accept-region selective risk, and we name it that way.
- **(b) Selective-risk envelope.** For the accept-region selective risk `R_Q(v)` used elsewhere, the certificate must be built on the *accepted* subsample and stated as a genuine uniform one-sided band for a ratio: either a finite grid of novelty levels with a union-bound multiplicity correction, or a monotone-constrained DKW-type band, with `eps(v)` the uniform-band width rather than a single-risk Hoeffding-Bentkus slack.

A plain RCPS / Hoeffding-Bentkus UCB certifies one scalar risk functional and does *not* by itself give a "for all `v`" statement; the uniform band is what pays for the continuum, and isotonic regression's cube-root pointwise behavior is absorbed by the band width. If monotonicity fails (a mid-novelty dip), drop to unconstrained Learn-then-Test with looser non-smooth bands.

*Proof strategy.* Isotonic regression against `v` (of marginal error in (a), of accepted-subsample error in (b)) removes the Holder-constant dependence; a union-bound or monotone-DKW construction converts the pointwise UCB into a uniform one-sided envelope. Monotonicity replaces the smoothness constant, so no bandwidth-bias term and no known-`L` assumption are needed.

*Assumptions.* Monotone risk in the chosen coordinate (directly checkable on RNP by regressing the error rate on max-Tanimoto / pocket-similarity / temporal bin, separately for the marginal and accepted-subsample curves, at the `(system, method)` unit); bounded loss. *Where it fails / risk:* monotonicity may fail; it is falsifiable up front, so it is the most defensible of the three. The main pitfall to avoid is conflating the marginal curve (a) with the selective-risk curve (b); we keep them distinct throughout. Note that monotonicity is *not* guaranteed on a cross-model-disagreement coordinate (see step 1), so T3 is stated over training-set-similarity axes.

**Label-starved tail: a computable lower bound, not the floor value.** `Delta_bar_h(v)` depends on `eta_Q`, the target error conditional, which is only estimable from accepted target labels in the window. Beyond the powered percentile `P` there are, by construction, too few accepted labels to identify `eta_Q`, so the *value* of `Delta_bar_h(v)` is unidentifiable exactly there. We therefore never promise to "report `Delta_bar_h(v)`" in the tail. Instead we deliver a *computable lower bound* on the local floor via a localized f-divergence / CVaR ambiguity ball (a continuous, per-`v` analog of the project's Thm 2): worst-case `Delta_bar_h(v)` over a divergence ball around the source conditional inside the window, which is computable from source labels plus a stated ball radius. The honest deliverable in the tail is "the local floor is at least this much" plus the statement that its exact value is unidentifiable. A stated-assumption monotone extrapolation of the `[0, P]` estimate is offered only as a labeled secondary, not as an identification.

#### Method & experiments

Assets, all reuse-first with near-zero GPU. **Independence unit (load-bearing):** the delivered RNP table has ~13,535 rows spanning 6 co-folding models over ~2,425 systems, so rows for the same system across models share the same ground-truth pose and the same `v` and are not exchangeable calibration points. The calibration unit is therefore *one prediction per (system, method)*. The primary continuous analysis is run **per model** (for AF3 alone, ~2,343 labeled independent systems). Any cross-model or E5 pooling is cluster-bootstrapped by `system_id`, and every effective-sample-size, `n_h`, `m_h`, and powered-range number is reported in *independent systems*, never in rows.

1. **Novelty coordinate.** Build `v` from RNP precomputed fields in `annotations.csv` / `all_similarity_scores.parquet`: max ligand Tanimoto (Morgan/ECFP4) as the primary axis, SuCOS pocket similarity + `qcov` and the temporal cutoff as alternatives.
